About the Project
East Africa's First Modern Expressway

The Thika Superhighway (officially the A2 Highway Improvement Project) is a 50km, 8-to-12 lane controlled-access expressway linking Nairobi's Central Business District to the industrial town of Thika. It forms part of the A2 Highway — the Cape to Cairo Road — connecting Namanga on the Tanzania-Kenya border to Moyale at the Ethiopian border.


Inaugurated on 9 November 2012 by President Mwai Kibaki, it became the first modern multi-lane divided highway in East Africa. Built at a cost of USD 360M (KSh 32B), it was funded by the African Development Bank, China Exim Bank and the Government of Kenya. Traffic surged from 85,000 vehicles per day in 2007 to 123,000 at completion — a 45% increase — and journey times fell from 2 hours to just 45 minutes.


Now in 2025–2026, the highway is undergoing active upgrades including new bus bays at Roysambu and Githurai, a new footbridge at Kihunguro, milling and resurfacing at Juja Highpoint, and a new parallel expressway announced by President Ruto in December 2025.

What Was Built

  • 14 grade-separated interchanges replacing dangerous roundabouts
  • Flyovers: Kasarani, Githurai, Globe Roundabout, Eastern Bypass
  • Underpasses: Kahawa, KU, Pangani, Survey of Kenya, Ruiru SC, Mang'u HS

Latest Research Updates
Thika Superhighway — Live 2025 & 2026 Developments
February 2026
KeNHA Orders Traders to Vacate for Bus Bay Construction
KeNHA issued a 7-day ultimatum to traders at Roysambu (both directions) and Githurai (Nairobi direction) to vacate road reserves for construction of dedicated bus bays. The bus bays will provide safe and orderly passenger pick-up and drop-off points, reducing illegal stops by matatus and PSVs and significantly easing congestion. Previous clearances in Kihunguro, Allsops, and Delview were conducted in the same programme.
Source: KeNHA Public Notice, Feb 9–24, 2026 · Kenyans.co.ke · Radio47
December 2025
President Ruto Announces Thika–Museum Hill Expressway
During Jamhuri Day celebrations at Nyayo Stadium on December 12, 2025, President William Ruto announced the construction of a new parallel expressway from Thika to Museum Hill (Nairobi CBD). He acknowledged the Thika Superhighway is now overwhelmed despite being a 6-lane dual carriageway and that the new expressway — similar to the JKIA Expressway — would be built in 2026 to eliminate heavy congestion.
Source: The Kenya Times, December 12, 2025 · State House Kenya
November 2025
Juja Highpoint (Centurion) Milling and Resurfacing Works
KeNHA conducted partial closures at the Juja Highpoint area (Centurion) in November 2025 to enable milling and resurfacing works on the superhighway. The works are part of the authority's long-term strategy to modernise transport infrastructure within the Nairobi Metropolitan Area and align with the Bus Rapid Transit (BRT) system and non-motorised transport lanes being developed.
Source: Kenyans.co.ke, November 2025
October 2025
Kihunguro Pedestrian Footbridge Under Construction
KeNHA announced a 2-month partial closure of the Kihunguro section (October 20 – December 20, 2025) to construct a new pedestrian footbridge. The bridge will eliminate a dangerous Zebra Crossing that had been causing major traffic jams by forcing vehicles to stop frequently during rush hours. Diversion routes were provided via the Total Petrol Station (Nairobi-bound) and Shell Petrol Station (Thika-bound).
Source: The Star, October 14, 2025 · KeNHA Official Notice
2024
Thika Superhighway Listed for Toll Stations — Study Underway
KeNHA unveiled plans to introduce toll stations on several major highways, with the Thika Superhighway listed alongside the Nairobi Southern Bypass, Nairobi-Nakuru-Mau Summit Highway, Kenol-Sagana-Marua Road, Mombasa Southern Bypass, and Dongo Kundu Bypass. A feasibility study is underway to assess viable toll point locations and revenue collection systems.
Source: Eastleigh Voice / KeNHA, August 2024
Ongoing
KSh 5B Thika Town Industrial Smart City Road Expansion
The National Government, in partnership with Kiambu County and Governor Kimani Wamatangi, is implementing a KSh 5B road expansion project in Thika Town. This includes upgrading Garissa Road into a 25km dual carriageway from the Thika Superhighway overpass to Makutano, two new interchanges at the Gatitu junction, doubling the Thika-Gatanga road, and constructing a new bridge near Blue Posts Hotel. Environmental impact assessments are complete and design is underway.
Source: BuildMart Africa · Kiambu County Government

Project Timeline
From Groundbreaking 2009 to Active Upgrades 2026
January 2009
Construction Begins
All three contractors mobilise simultaneously. Globe Roundabout flyover excavation marks the visible start. This was Kenya's most ambitious road project at the time.
2010 – 2011
Flyovers Rise Above Nairobi
Kasarani, Githurai, and Globe Roundabout flyovers completed. Nairobi's skyline changes — national excitement builds rapidly as Kenyans see the transformation from just 8-lane expansions.
July 2012
Substantial Completion
All three lots achieve practical completion — on schedule. The road opens to traffic in rolling phases. Commuters immediately feel the transformation: 2 hours becomes 45 minutes.
9 November 2012
Grand Presidential Inauguration
President Mwai Kibaki officially inaugurates East Africa's first superhighway in a national celebration. Kenya sets a new benchmark for African infrastructure. Total cost: USD 360M.
2013 – 2020
Economic Corridor Boom
Thika Road Mall, Garden City, Ridgeways Mall, TRM, Kasarani Mall open. Property values rise up to 10×. Industrial and logistics hubs expand rapidly. 150,000+ daily vehicles by 2020.
October – December 2025
Kihunguro Footbridge Works
KeNHA partially closes Kihunguro section to construct new pedestrian footbridge. Diversion routes established at Total and Shell petrol stations. Expected to eliminate dangerous Zebra Crossing jams.
December 2025
🎙️ Ruto Announces New Thika Expressway
On Jamhuri Day, President Ruto announces a new expressway from Thika to Museum Hill (Nairobi CBD) to ease overwhelming congestion on the Superhighway, similar to the JKIA Expressway model. Launch planned for 2026.
February 2026 — Active Now
Bus Bay Construction Begins (Roysambu & Githurai)
KeNHA construction of new designated bus bays at Roysambu and Githurai begins after trader evictions. Part of the broader BRT and urban mobility strategy for Nairobi Metropolitan Area.
