About
Project Overview

The Riruta–Ngong Metre Gauge Railway (MGR) is a new 12.5km commuter rail line branching from the existing Nairobi commuter network at Riruta and running through Karen and Bulbul to Ngong town in Kajiado County. Officially launched by President Ruto on 15 December 2023, it is the first new commuter rail line added to Nairobi's network in decades and is being constructed by China Road and Bridge Corporation (CRBC).


The project directly addresses chronic congestion on Ngong Road — one of Nairobi's most gridlocked corridors. On completion, the line will carry 10,000 passengers daily, reducing peak-hour commutes from nearly 2 hours to just 30 minutes and cutting transport costs by 50%. A signature engineering feature is a 3km bridge from the Southern Bypass to Karen Shopping Centre. Phase 2 will extend the line to Kiserian, and Phase 3 to Ongata Rongai, forming a full metropolitan southwest loop.

Key Features
What's Included
  • 12.5km metre-gauge railway — new branch off existing Nairobi commuter MGR
  • 4 stations: Riruta, Karen, Bulbul, and Ngong terminus
  • 3km elevated bridge from Southern Bypass to Karen Shopping Centre
  • 10,000 passengers per day capacity when fully operational
  • Reduces Riruta–Ngong commute from 2 hours to just 30 minutes
  • 50% reduction in daily transport costs for thousands of households
  • Phase 2: Extension to Kiserian | Phase 3: Extension to Ongata Rongai
  • Aligns with Nairobi Transport Masterplan and Vision 2030
