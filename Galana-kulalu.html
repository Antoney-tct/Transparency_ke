About
Project Overview

The Galana Kulalu Food Security Project is Kenya's most ambitious irrigation initiative — a Vision 2030 flagship spanning 1.75 million acres of the Galana and Kulalu ranches in Kilifi and Tana River Counties. Its target is to bring 250,000 acres under commercial irrigation, producing up to 14 million bags of maize annually and ending Kenya's perennial 10-million-bag annual maize deficit.


Implemented by the National Irrigation Authority (NIA) through a PPP framework, the project saw a major breakthrough in 2025 with completion of the irrigation canal and reservoir infrastructure (KSh 519.4M). Private investors Selu Limited (20,000 acres) and Al Dahra Group of UAE (180,000 acres, USD 800M pledge) are engaged. The Athi Dam contract was signed 30 December 2025 with China Communications Construction Company — the critical water storage infrastructure that will unlock the full 250,000-acre irrigation potential.

Key Features
What's Included
  • 1.75 million acres of Galana and Kulalu ranches — largest in E&S Africa
  • 250,000 acres target under commercial irrigation via Athi Dam
  • 753m inlet canal, 450M-litre reservoir, 1,210m outlet canal — completed 2025
  • Athi Dam (300MCM) contracted to CCCC — December 30, 2025
  • KSh 2.9B electrification by REREC to replace costly diesel pumping
  • Al Dahra UAE: USD 800M commitment for 200,000 acres of farming
  • Selu Ltd: 20,000 acres — maize and soybean pilot trials completed
  • 14 million bags of maize annually at full capacity (vs 10M bag deficit)
