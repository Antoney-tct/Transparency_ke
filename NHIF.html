NHIF Reforms

Ministry: Health Launched: 2022 Target Completion: 2027
Reform Progress
45%
Implementation Phase

The NHIF Reforms represent a comprehensive overhaul of Kenya's health insurance system to achieve Universal Health Coverage (UHC) under the government's Big Four Agenda. The reforms aim to make quality healthcare affordable and accessible to all Kenyans.

Key Reform Areas:

Enhanced Benefits Package

Expansion of covered services including chronic diseases, cancer treatment, and specialized surgeries

Progressive Contributions

Income-based contributions replacing flat rates to improve equity

Digital Transformation

New biometric systems and mobile platforms for seamless access

The reformed NHIF will serve as the primary health financing mechanism for Kenya's UHC program, with mandatory enrollment for all citizens and legal residents.

New NHIF Benefits

The reformed package covers these essential services:

Primary Care

Unlimited outpatient visits to accredited facilities

Inpatient Care

Full coverage for hospitalization including surgeries

Chronic Disease

Diabetes, hypertension, and cancer management

Maternity

Comprehensive prenatal to postnatal care

Mental Health

Included for the first time in NHIF coverage

Emergency

Road ambulance and emergency evacuation
