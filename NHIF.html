About the Programme
NHIF Universal Healthcare Reforms

The National Hospital Insurance Fund (NHIF) reforms represent a comprehensive transformation of Kenya's healthcare financing system, aimed at achieving Universal Health Coverage (UHC) for all 56 million citizens across all 47 counties.


Under the reformed system, benefit packages are significantly expanded to cover outpatient services, chronic disease management, maternity care, and oncology — previously not covered. The reforms also streamline claims processing to reduce delays faced by hospitals and patients alike.

Key Reforms
What Has Changed
  • Expanded outpatient coverage for all registered members
  • Chronic disease management (diabetes, hypertension, cancer)
  • Free maternity services at all public facilities
  • Enhanced reimbursement rates to hospitals
  • Digital claims processing reducing turnaround to 7 days
  • Community Health Financing to cover informal sector
  • Integration with SHA (Social Health Authority)
