About
Project Overview

The Lamu Port-South Sudan-Ethiopia Transport (LAPSSET) Corridor is Kenya's largest infrastructure project under Vision 2030, officially launched on 2 March 2012 by Presidents Kibaki, Kiir, and PM Zenawi. Designed to create Kenya's second transport corridor, LAPSSET will link Lamu Port on the Indian Ocean to landlocked South Sudan (Juba) and Ethiopia (Addis Ababa) through 32 deep-water port berths, 1,500km of highways, 1,700km of standard gauge railway, and oil pipelines.


The anchor project — Lamu Port at Manda Bay — has delivered four operational berths by 2024. Cargo volumes have surged from just 25,000 metric tonnes in 2018 to 799,161 tonnes in 2025, a 3,097% increase. The first 88km of the 410km LAPSSET road (Lamu to Isiolo) was completed by October 2025 with KSh 28B budgeted. The port is transitioning to a Public Limited Liability Company, signalling a new commercial phase of operations.

Key Features
What's Included
  • 32-berth deep-sea port at Manda Bay — Post-Suezmax capable
  • 4 berths fully operational (2024); cargo volumes growing 14× since 2018
  • 1,500km LAPSSET highway to Juba and Addis Ababa
  • 1,700km standard gauge railway — Lamu to Ethiopia and South Sudan
  • Crude oil pipeline from Lokichar, Turkana to Lamu Port
  • Three international airports: Lamu, Isiolo, and Lodwar (Turkana)
  • Three resort cities: Lamu Island, Isiolo, and Lake Turkana
  • Shorter route to Ethiopia (saves 1,000km vs via Djibouti)
