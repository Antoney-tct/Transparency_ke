About
Project Overview

Kisumu Port is the largest lake port in East Africa, a critical transit hub for cargo across Lake Victoria connecting Kenya, Uganda, and Tanzania. After decades of near-dormancy due to deteriorating railway links, water hyacinth invasion, and operational neglect, the port was rehabilitated at a cost of KSh 3 billion from 2018, with spectacular results — cargo volumes have grown from just 25,000 metric tonnes in 2018 to 496,516 tonnes in 2025, a 1,886% increase.


The Kenya Ports Authority (KPA) is now building on this momentum with a new Kisumu Port Resilient Infrastructure Project (in partnership with TradeMark Africa) delivering a modern passenger terminal with 1,000-person/hour capacity, new cargo warehouse, immigration, and customs facilities. In early 2026, the National Treasury allocated KSh 350M for dredging to deepen the port basin to -7 metres, enabling larger vessels and higher cargo volumes. MV Uhuru II — Kenya's first locally assembled vessel — is now operational on the lake.

Key Features
What's Included
  • 4 operational berths with 400m quay and 4m draught at the pier
  • MV Uhuru II — Kenya's first assembled vessel, 1,800t, 14 knots
  • Oil jetty serving petroleum tankers to Uganda (MT Kabaka Mutebi III)
  • New passenger terminal: 1,000 passengers/hour capacity (in design)
  • Modern cargo warehouse to complement passenger facilities
  • KSh 350M dredging to deepen basin to -7m for larger vessels
  • SGR extension Naivasha–Kisumu (263.7km) with 8km port branch planned
  • Satellite landing: Sio Pier, Port Victoria, Asembo Bay, Kendu Bay
