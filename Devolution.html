Kenya's Devolution

Kenya's devolved system of government — established under the 2010 Constitution — distributes political, administrative, and fiscal power to 47 county governments, bringing governance closer to citizens across all regions.

Devolution Framework
Three Core Pillars of Devolution
01
Political Devolution
Elected county governors and assemblies make decisions locally. Citizens elect representatives at ward, constituency, and county levels. The 2010 Constitution guarantees democratic governance at all levels with clear separation of national and county powers.
02
Administrative Devolution
County governments independently manage their own public service, county assembly service, and institutions. Each county has a Governor, Deputy Governor, County Assembly with elected MCAs, and a full executive committee overseeing devolved departments.
03
Fiscal Devolution
Counties receive at least 15% of national revenue as equitable share (currently ~KSh 385B in FY25). They also raise own-source revenue and receive conditional grants. CRA determines the sharing formula based on population, poverty, land area, and basic services.

Devolution Achievements
Impact Since 2013
4,000+ Health Facilities Upgraded
Counties have constructed and upgraded health centres, dispensaries, and referral hospitals, dramatically improving primary healthcare access across rural areas.
50,000km of County Roads
Counties manage over 50,000km of rural access roads, opening up agricultural areas, reducing post-harvest losses and enabling market connectivity for farmers.
Universal ECDE Enrolment
Counties have constructed over 30,000 ECDE classrooms nationally and dramatically increased pre-primary enrolment, setting children up for better educational outcomes.
Water Infrastructure
Borehole drilling, water pan construction, and piped water schemes have expanded clean water access to millions of Kenyans previously dependent on unsafe sources.
Subsidised Farm Inputs
County governments have provided subsidised seeds, fertiliser, and extension services to smallholder farmers, boosting food production and rural incomes significantly.
Local Democracy Deepened
Over 1,450 ward MCAs, 47 governors, and 47 senators provide democratic representation at the most local level, giving citizens direct accountability mechanisms.
