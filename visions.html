National Development Blueprint · 2008 – 2030 & Beyond

Kenya's Vision & Future

Kenya Vision 2030 is the country's official long-term development blueprint aiming to transform Kenya into a globally competitive, middle-income nation by 2030, with a high quality of life for all citizens.

"A globally competitive and prosperous Kenya with a high quality of life by 2030"

Kenya Vision 2030 is anchored on three pillars — economic, social, and political — implemented through five-year Medium Term Plans. It targets 10% annual GDP growth, universal health coverage, and a fully industrialising middle-income economy.

Vision 2030 Framework
The Three Pillars
01
Economic Pillar
Achieves 10% annual GDP growth through focus on 6 sectors: tourism, agriculture, manufacturing, financial services, wholesale & retail, and Business Process Outsourcing. Kenya is now East Africa's largest economy.
Economic Pillar Progress75%
02
Social Pillar
Invests in people through education, health, environment, housing, and gender equity. CBC curriculum, UHC, affordable housing, and water for all are flagship programmes targeting equitable quality of life across all 47 counties.
Social Pillar Progress68%
03
Political Pillar
Promotes democratic governance, rule of law, security, and transparency. The 2010 Constitution, devolution to 47 counties, anti-corruption reforms, and strengthened oversight institutions form the governance backbone.
Political Pillar Progress72%
