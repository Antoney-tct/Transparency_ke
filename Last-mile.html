About
Project Overview

The Last Mile Connectivity Project (LMCP) is Kenya's flagship rural electrification programme, launched in 2015 with a mission to connect every household within 600 metres of an existing transformer to the national electricity grid. Over a decade, it has transformed Kenya from 27% electricity access in 2013 to over 76% in 2024 — one of the world's fastest electrification rates — earning recognition as a global benchmark for achieving SDG 7 (affordable clean energy for all).


Implemented by Kenya Power (KPLC) and REREC, financed in four phases by the World Bank, AfDB, French Development Agency (AFD), European Union, and European Investment Bank (EIB), the programme has connected 9.6 million households by 2024. Phase 4 (KSh 27B, signed May 2024) targets 280,000 additional households across 32 counties, with EU Ambassador praising Kenya as "on track to achieving its electricity connectivity goals and recognised as a leader in clean energy."

Key Features
What's Included
  • Connects households within 600m of existing transformers to the grid
  • 9.6 million households on the national grid as of 2024
  • Access rate raised from 27% to 76% in just over a decade
  • Phase 4 (May 2024): KSh 27B to connect 280,000 homes across 32 counties
  • Subsidised connection fee reduced from KSh 35,000 to KSh 15,000
  • Power lines carry fibre optic cables for simultaneous internet access
  • Daily commissioning of new connections across the country (2025)
  • Target: 100% national electricity access by 2030
