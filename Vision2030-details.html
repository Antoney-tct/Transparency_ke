About Vision 2030
Kenya's Long-Term Development Blueprint

Kenya Vision 2030 is the country's official long-term development blueprint, covering the period 2008 to 2030. It aims to transform Kenya into a newly industrialising, globally competitive, middle-income country providing a high quality of life to all its citizens in a clean and secure environment.


The Vision is implemented through successive five-year Medium Term Plans (MTPs). MTP I (2008–2012) focused on foundations; MTP II (2013–2017) accelerated infrastructure; MTP III (2018–2022) tackled the Big Four; and MTP IV (2023–2027) is currently underway, targeting the Bottom-Up Economic Transformation Agenda (BETA).

2030 Goals
  • 10% annual GDP growth rate sustained

  • Universal health coverage for all Kenyans

  • Zero extreme poverty — inclusive growth
Economic Pillar
Aims at achieving 10% annual GDP growth, focusing on tourism, agriculture, wholesale & retail, manufacturing, and financial services as key sectors. Kenya's economy has grown significantly under this pillar, becoming East Africa's largest economy.
GDP Growth Target75%
Social Pillar
Invests in people through education, health, environment, housing, and gender equality. Initiatives include CBC curriculum reforms, UHC, affordable housing, and environmental conservation, targeting equity and quality of life improvement for all Kenyans.
Social Development Index68%
Political Pillar
Focuses on democratic governance, the rule of law, security, and transparency. Key reforms include the 2010 Constitution, devolution of power to 47 counties, anti-corruption measures, and strengthening of oversight institutions.
