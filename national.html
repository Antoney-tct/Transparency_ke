Constitutional Framework · Chapter 8
Three Arms of National Government
Article 130 🏛️
The Executive
Executive authority is vested in the President as Head of State, Head of Government, and Commander-in-Chief. The Executive implements laws, manages state affairs, and directs Kenya's development agenda through 22 Cabinet Secretaries and the 26 Principal Secretaries.
President — 5-year term, maximum 2 terms

Article 93 ⚖️
The Legislature
Bicameral Parliament comprising the National Assembly (350 members) and Senate (67 members). Parliament enacts national legislation, approves the national budget (KSh 3.99T in FY25), and provides constitutional oversight of the Executive and state organs.
National Assembly — 290 constituency + 47 women + 12 nominated + Speaker
Senate — 47 elected + 16 women + 4 youth/PWD nominated

Article 159 🔨
The Judiciary
Judicial authority is derived from the people and vested in the courts. Completely independent of Executive and Legislature. Safeguards the 2010 Constitution, rule of law, and fundamental rights of every Kenyan — including socio-economic rights under Articles 43–47.

Article 248 — Chapter Fifteen
Constitutional Commissions & Independent Offices
IEBC — Independent Electoral & Boundaries Commission
Organises and manages elections and referenda. Delimits constituency and ward boundaries. Registers all voters and ensures free and fair elections across all 47 counties.
EACC — Ethics & Anti-Corruption Commission
Investigates and recommends prosecution for corruption. Conducts asset declarations for all public officers. Educates Kenyans on integrity. Key institution for governance transparency.
Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA)
Collects taxes and customs duties on behalf of the national government. Collected KSh 2.49T in FY25 (+22.9% YoY). Manages iTax, customs clearance and compliance programmes.
Auditor-General
Audits ALL public accounts — national and 47 county governments. Annual reports tabled in Parliament. Ensures public money is used lawfully and for its intended purpose.
National Cohesion & Integration Commission
Promotes peaceful coexistence among Kenya's 44+ communities. Monitors hate speech, incitement and discrimination. Ensures political discourse remains within constitutional boundaries.
Commission on Revenue Allocation (CRA)
Determines the equitable share of national revenue allocated to the 47 county governments (currently KSh 385B/year). Uses formula based on population, poverty, land area and basic services.
Presidential Priorities 2025–2027
Four National Priority Areas (State of Nation Nov 2025)
  • Education & Human Capital — Budget raised from KSh 490B (2021) to KSh 700B+ (2025). 76,000 teachers hired by Dec 2024. CBC curriculum, STEM and innovation focus.
  • Transport & Logistics — 2,500 highways mapped for dualling + 28,000km to be tarmacked in 10 years. New Thika Expressway, LAPSSET, SGR expansion.
  • Energy & Irrigation — 100% electricity access target. Last Mile Phase 4 (280,000 households). Galana Kulalu 250,000-acre irrigation scheme.
  • Science, Research & Innovation — State Dept for Science, Research & Innovation established. R&D budget target: 2% of GDP (currently 0.8%). KSh 1 trillion innovation fund over 10 years.
  • Affordable Housing (BETA) — 240,000 units under construction in 111 constituencies. 480,000+ jobs created. Rent-to-own from KSh 3,900/month.
  • Food Security & Agriculture — Maize production: 34M bags (2022) → 47.6M bags (2023) → 70M bags projected (2025). SHA universal healthcare 27M+ registered.
