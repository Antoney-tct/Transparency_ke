Our Purpose
Mission & Vision of Kenya
Our Mission
To serve all Kenyans with integrity, empower citizens through devolved governance, and drive inclusive prosperity through innovative, accountable, and transparent leadership that leaves no county behind — from Mandera to Mombasa, from Marsabit to Narok.
Our Vision — 2030
A globally competitive and prosperous Kenya with a high quality of life for all citizens by 2030. We aim to be a middle-income country with a clean, secure, and sustainable environment — building on the momentum of devolution, digital transformation, and landmark infrastructure investments.

Our Values
The Principles That Guide Kenya
Enshrined in Article 10 of the 2010 Constitution — national values and principles of governance that bind all state organs, public officers, and citizens.
Transparency
Open, accountable governance in all government operations, procurement, and decision-making. Citizens have the right to information.
Rule of Law
The 2010 Constitution is supreme. No person is above the law — from the President to every citizen. Judicial independence is guaranteed.
Inclusivity
Every Kenyan participates in and benefits from national development regardless of ethnicity, gender, region, or economic status.
Innovation
Embracing technology, research, and creative solutions — from eCitizen to the Silicon Savannah — to solve Kenya's development challenges.
Sustainability
Development that meets the needs of today without compromising tomorrow. Kenya leads Africa in renewable energy and conservation.
Harambee
"Let's Pull Together" — Kenya's founding principle of collective effort, community self-help, and national unity across all 44 communities.

National Achievements
What Makes Kenya Remarkable
Renewable Energy Leader
93% of electricity from renewables (geothermal, hydro, wind, solar). Lake Turkana Wind Power is Africa's largest wind farm at 310MW. Olkaria geothermal is world's largest (792MW).
Mobile Money Pioneer
M-Pesa, invented in Kenya 2007, is the world's most successful mobile money platform with 50M+ users across Africa. Kenya processes more money via mobile than any country on Earth.
World's Running Capital
Kenya has won more Olympic medals in distance running than any nation. Eliud Kipchoge holds the marathon world record. Iten town hosts the world's greatest distance runners.
Silicon Savannah
Nairobi is Africa's leading tech hub — home to Google, Microsoft, IBM, Safaricom, Andela and 300+ startups. Konza Technopolis (80% complete) will be Africa's smart city model.
Regional Gateway
Mombasa Port handles 30M+ tonnes/year for East and Central Africa. JKIA is East Africa's busiest airport. LAPSSET will connect Kenya to Ethiopia, Uganda, and South Sudan.
Wildlife Conservation
Home to the Great Wildebeest Migration — the greatest wildlife spectacle on Earth. Tsavo is the world's largest national park complex. Kenya earns $2B+ annually from tourism and conservation.
2010 Constitution
Voted 67% in favour. Considered one of Africa's most progressive constitutions — guarantees socio-economic rights (food, water, health, education), devolution, and an independent judiciary.
UN & Diplomatic Hub
Nairobi hosts the UN Environment Programme (UNEP) and UN-Habitat — the only African city hosting two UN headquarters. Kenya is founding member of the African Union.
Infrastructure Legacy
SGR (KSh 327B, 2017), Thika Superhighway (2012), Nairobi Expressway (2022), Dongo Kundu Bypass (2024) — Kenya has the most ambitious infrastructure programme in sub-Saharan Africa.
Our History
Kenya — A Timeline of a Nation
1895
British East Africa Protectorate
Britain establishes the East Africa Protectorate. Uganda Railway (Mombasa-Kisumu) constructed using Indian labour — "the Lunatic Express."
1920
Kenya Colony Established
British Crown Colony formally created. Land alienation intensifies, displacing Kikuyu farmers from the White Highlands and fuelling future resistance.
1944
Kenya African Union (KAU) Founded
The first major nationalist movement, led by Jomo Kenyatta from 1947. The movement demands land reform, better wages, and civil rights for Africans.
1952–1960
Mau Mau Uprising
The Land and Freedom Army (Mau Mau) fights British colonialism. Kenyatta imprisoned. The uprising — though suppressed — accelerates the path to independence.
12 December 1963
INDEPENDENCE — Uhuru!
Kenya gains independence from Britain. Jomo Kenyatta becomes the first Prime Minister. The Kenyan flag is raised at midnight to jubilant crowds across the nation.
1964
Republic of Kenya
Kenya becomes a Republic on 12 December 1964. Jomo Kenyatta becomes first President. Harambee — "Let's Pull Together" — adopted as the national motto.
1978–2002
Moi Era & Multi-Party Return
Daniel arap Moi leads Kenya until 2002. Multi-party democracy restored in 1991 under international pressure. Opposition movements grow throughout the 1990s.
2002
Democratic Transition
Mwai Kibaki wins historic election ending 24 years of KANU rule. Peaceful democratic transition celebrated across the country as a milestone for Africa.
2010
New Constitution Adopted
Kenyans vote 67% in favour of a new progressive Constitution — establishing 47 county governments, a bicameral parliament, and comprehensive bill of rights.
2013
Devolution Begins
First governors and county assemblies elected. Power, resources, and governance devolved to 47 counties for the first time. KSh 190B allocated in Year 1.
2022 — Present
Kenya Kwanza Era & Bottom-Up Agenda
William Ruto wins the presidency with the Bottom-Up Economic Transformation Agenda (BETA). Focus: affordable housing, agriculture, healthcare, education, and the digital economy.

Geography & Nature
Kenya's Physical Landscape
  • Mt Kenya (5,199m) — Africa's second highest peak and a UNESCO World Heritage Site
  • Lake Victoria — Africa's largest lake shared with Uganda and Tanzania
  • Great Rift Valley — runs north-south through Kenya, dotted with lakes and volcanoes
  • Lake Turkana — world's largest permanent desert lake (Jade Sea)
  • Maasai Mara — hosts the world's greatest wildlife migration every year
  • 500km Indian Ocean coastline from Vanga (Tanzania border) to Kiunga (Somalia border)
  • Climate: Alpine (Mt Kenya), semi-arid (North), tropical coast, savannah (interior)
  • Forests cover 7% — Mau, Mt Kenya, Aberdares are key water towers for East Africa
Economy & Trade
Key Economic Sectors
  • Agriculture: Tea, coffee, horticulture — 26% of GDP. Kenya is world's largest black tea exporter.
  • Tourism: $2B+ annually. Maasai Mara, Amboseli, Diani Beach, Mount Kenya.
  • Remittances: $4.95B in 2024 — fastest growing forex earner after tourism.
  • ICT & Fintech: M-Pesa, Safaricom, 300+ tech startups. KSh 14B eCitizen revenues (2024).
  • Manufacturing: EPZA zones, textiles, food processing, cement, and pharmaceuticals.
  • Logistics: Mombasa Port — handles 30M+ tonnes. JKIA — East Africa's busiest airport.
  • Energy: 93% renewables. Geothermal (792MW), Wind (310MW), Solar, Hydro.
  • Real Estate: Fastest growing market in East Africa — Nairobi ranked top investment hub.
