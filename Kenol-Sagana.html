About
Project Overview

The Kenol–Sagana–Marua Highway is an 84km dual carriageway expansion of the A2 road through Murang'a, Kirinyaga, and Nyeri counties — a critical segment of the Great North Road/Trans-African Highway No. 4, linking Cape Town to Cairo. Jointly funded by the African Development Bank (AfDB) and the Government of Kenya at KSh 16.7B (USD 155M), the project transforms a dangerous single carriageway into a modern 4-lane divided highway.


Contracted to China's Jiangxi Engineering Group and overseen by KeNHA, works began October 2020 in two lots: Lot 1 (48km Kenol–Sagana, KSh 8.49B) and Lot 2 (36km Sagana–Marua, KSh 6.1B). Lot 1 is 100% complete and open; Lot 2 is at 90% overall, with the Marua interchange section delayed by compensation disputes affecting 2,365 project-affected persons in Kirinyaga and Nyeri. Full completion is now targeted for January 2026.

Key Features
What's Included
  • 84km single carriageway upgraded to 4-lane divided dual carriageway
  • Part of the Great North Road — Cape Town to Cairo Trans-African Hwy
  • Major interchanges at Kenol, Makutano, Sagana, Karatina, and Marua
  • Emergency trauma centre to be built along the corridor
  • Non-motorised transport (NMT) lanes for cyclists and pedestrians
  • Reduces Nairobi–Nyeri travel time from 3 hours to under 90 minutes
  • Connects Northern Corridor (Mombasa) to LAPSSET (via Isiolo)
  • Boosts agriculture, manufacturing, and tourism in the Central Region
