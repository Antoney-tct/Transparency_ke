About
Project Overview

The Dongo Kundu Bypass (officially the Mombasa Southern Bypass Highway) is a 17.5km dual carriageway that connects Mombasa Mainland West (Miritini) to Mombasa Mainland South (Ng'ombeni), completely bypassing congested Mombasa Island. Opened to the public in August 2024, it is the most expensive road per kilometre in Kenya's history at approximately KSh 2.7B per km.


Funded 80% by Japan International Cooperation Agency (JICA) through concessional loans at 1.2% annual interest over 30 years, and 20% by the Government of Kenya, the KSh 40B bypass features three landmark structures: the 1,440-metre Mteza Bridge (longest bridge in Kenya and East Africa, seven times longer than Nyali Bridge), the 660-metre Mwache Bridge, and the 690-metre Tsunza Viaduct. Travel time from SGR Miritini to Diani has been cut from 2.5 hours to just 45 minutes.

Key Features
What's Included
  • 17.5km dual carriageway — bypasses Mombasa Island entirely
  • 1,440m Mteza Bridge — longest bridge in Kenya and East Africa
  • 660m Mwache Bridge spanning Port Reitz Creek
  • 690m Tsunza Viaduct through the Tsunza peninsula
  • Cloverleaf interchanges at Miritini and Kipevu
  • 1.3km direct spur road to Moi International Airport
  • Connects directly to SGR Miritini terminus for seamless logistics
  • Anchors 3,000-acre Dongo Kundu Special Economic Zone (SEZ)
